Interesting facts about Camel:

  • There are two types of Camel: One humped or 'dromedary' camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have long eyelashes which helps them to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels can travel upto 40 miles per hour.

Interesting facts about Chameleon:

  • Chameleons have over 200 species.
  • Almost half of their population lives in Madagascar.
  • Most chameleons change their colour with the help of the crystals on their body and are able to signal other chameleons.

Intersting facts about Pigeon:

  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  • Pigeons can find their way back to the nest from 1300 miles away.
  • During the ancient times, pigeons were used as messengers and they delivered letters to the people.

Interesting facts about Teddy bear:

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.
  • The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall.

Interesting facts about Panda:

  • Pandas are lazy. Eating and sleeping makes their day.
  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • An adult panda can eat 12-38 kilos of bamboo per day.

Interesting facts about Cicada:

  • Cicadas exixt in more that 3000 species.
  • They can buzz louder than a lawnmower.
  • Their wings repel water and bacteria.
